Desert Vista TV

Learn more!

Desert Vista Thunder Vision is a student-centered media club, which covers DVHS news, sports, and events. The club covers Desert Vista High School athletic games online, creates videos for the school video boards and school events, and is responsible for the student announcements. In addition, DVTV assists the TUHSD district marketing and public relations departments to promote the Thunder to assist in its institutional advancement needs. Students of all talent levels are welcome. The Reel Thunder Filmmakers teams also create and produce works for monthly challenges and competitions. This group allows students with an interest in journalism, film, sports reporting, etc. to gain valuable hands-on experience to take to college and beyond. Club members attend workshops, conventions, and competitions. Students learn what it takes to be part of a production team of on-air personalities, producers, directors, editors, etc. Students also learn basic reporter skills in putting together news stories.


Meeting Location: A156


Meeting Times: DVTV: Mondays from 7:30 a.m. to 8:00 a.m.

Reel Thunder Filmmakers: Fridays from 3:30 p.m to 4 p.m.

DV Underwater Robotics

Learn more!

The Desert Vista Underwater Robotics club is all about submarines. There are three teams that make up this club, the beginner team, the for-fun team and the competition team. All new members will join the beginner team, where they will be taught how to design, build, code and drive submarines. The for-fun team is for experienced members how want to build a submarine for no other purpose than to have fun with it. The competition team is our most advanced team, which is made up of our most dedicated and experienced members who design their own submarine to compete in two international competitions.

Model UN

Learn more!

A club that serves to model the inner workings of the United Nations whereby the students participating are diplomats of a chosen country and of a particular committee (and their own individual topics) by again their own choice of the United Nations. The focus is on improving the students diplomatic, leadership, and cooperation skills with an emphasis of teamwork and collaboration with others, rather than individual work, and to garner a more deeper understanding of world issues such as environmental deterioration, refugee crisis, state sovereignty and many others both broadly as a topic at whole as well as on a country by country basis.


Meeting Location: A112


Meeting Times: Tuesdays afterschool from 3:26-4:10
